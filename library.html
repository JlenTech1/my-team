Kapasa Makasa University Library

An independent university library offering diverse academic resources since June 2021

About the Library

Kapasa Makasa University is located in Mulakupikwa village along the Great North Road in Chinsali District of Muchinga Province, Zambia. The library started operating in June 2019 as a branch of the Copperbelt University, and since June 2021, it functions independently under the leadership of a dedicated Librarian.

It plays a central role in the academic life of the university, offering students, researchers, and the wider community access to essential academic resources within a welcoming and well-equipped environment.

Library Aim and Objectives

Mission: To provide information resources aligned with the teaching, learning, research, and community service goals of the University.

Vision: To be the center of academic activity through relevant and timely information services.

Objective: To supply high-quality information materials to students and staff.

Library Services

1. Outreach Services

KMU Library conducts outreach services to nearby secondary and primary schools during events such as World Book and Copyright Day, International Literacy Day, and other significant celebrations. These initiatives aim to promote literacy, raise awareness about copyright issues, and foster a passion for reading and learning within the community.

2. Special Collections

KMU Library Special Collections include important resources such as rare books, theses, reports, audiovisual items, and distinctive periodicals. These materials are carefully safeguarded, with controlled access.

3. Internet Access

The Internet access section provides users with access to global digital repositories, databases like Astria Digital Library (ADL), MyLoft, and others for academic research.

4. Reader Services

KMU Library provides users with quiet, focused study and reading environments, featuring comfortable seating, proper lighting, and a distraction-free atmosphere.

5. Lending Services

Library lending services allow users to borrow materials such as books, research projects, and past examination papers for a set period of time.

6. Information Literacy

Information literacy services teach students the skills needed to search for, assess, and utilize information, including library tours and familiarization with rules.

7. Children’s Corner

A specially designed area for young readers, offering educational materials and fostering literacy among children through outreach initiatives and school collaborations.

8. Reference Service

Reference services help users locate materials, answer questions, and provide research support, available in-person and online.
